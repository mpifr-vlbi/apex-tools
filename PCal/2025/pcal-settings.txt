

=== Tone Synthesizer - Web Access ===

Web GUI of tone synthesizer:
  http://10.0.6.249/display

Command entry:
  http://10.0.6.249/scpi

  FREQ <mhz>
  POW <nn.n> dBm
  OUTPUT [on|off]

Spectrometer view (XFFTS) on VNC:
  vnc server:  ffts1:2
  passwd:      ... ask staff...

Two harmonic mixers (HM) for tone generation:
 - HM for NFLASH (230G/260G VLBI)
 - HM for SEPIA (345G VLBI)
 - future: Schottky mixer for 86 GHz VLBI tone

Both HM have attached 3+10 dB (13 dB) of attenuation.
This prevents frying the HMs when inadvertently
setting the synthesizer to maximum output power.

Synthesizer power should be ramped up/down in
small steps to be delicate to the HMs.

The EHT settings below all produce a tone at
  ~423.0 MHz  in recorded EHT Band 4 of 230/260/345G VLBI
provided that the DBBC3 Valon synth is tuned accordingly,
  cf. DBBC3-Valon-Tuning.txt
The GMVA settings produce a tone at 19 MHz in VDIF channel 0,
and FPT (86/260 GHz) settings dual tones one at 249 MHz and
a secondary tone at 252 MHz.

=== 230G EHT VLBI ===

The HM must have 10+3 dB attenuators for the settings below.

Command sequence for http://10.0.6.249/scpi to enable tone in band 4:

pow -20 dbm
freq 15315.0 mhz
output on
pow 0 dbm
pow 10 dbm
pow 15 dbm
pow 19 dbm
pow 21 dbm

  Tone should be visible on XFFTS screen #5 at ~3500 MHz

Disable tone:

pow 20 dbm
pow 15 dbm
pow 0 dbm
pow -20 dbm
output off

Alternate pcal tuning (from 2024 track e24a08) to place tone in band 3:

pow -20 dbm
freq 15315.0 mhz
output on
pow 0 dbm
pow 15 dbm
pow 22 dbm
pow 22.5 dbm
pow 22.30 dbm

  Tone should be visible on XFFTS screen #6 at ~2500 MHz

=== 345G EHT VLBI ===

The HM must have 10+3 dB attenuators for the settings below.

Command sequence for http://10.0.6.249/scpi to enable tone in band 4:

pow -20 dbm
freq 14009.0 mhz
output on
pow 0 dbm
pow 15 dbm
pow 18 dbm
pow 22 dbm
pow 23.6 dbm

  Tone should be visible on XFFTS screen #8 at ~550 MHz
  (was: Tone should be visible on XFFTS screen #5 at ~3500 MHz)

  Power into the HM is effectively 10.6 dBm after attenuators.

Disable tone:

pow 15 dbm
pow 0 dbm
pow -20 dbm
output off

For analog coherence tests the reference tone can be configured with:

[oper@cc-apex ~]$ remoteAnritsuMG.py freq 422.9900 mhz pow 9.0 dbm output on

In digital coherence tests (Mark6 recording) the tone can be checked with:

oper@recorder4:~> m5tone.py /mark6-4_fuse/12/<scan name>.vdif VDIF_8192-8192-1-2 tmp.m5tone 1 1e-3 423e6 819200 0



=== 86G GMVA VLBI ===

The HM is a tone generator assembly with built-in amplifier.
The synthesizer driving it is separate from the EHT tone synth.

Command sequence for http://10.0.6.230/scpi to enable tone:

pow -20 dbm
freq 17219 MHz
output on
pow -10 dbm
pow -5 dbm
pow 0 dbm
pow 4.5 dbm

Disable tone:

pow 0 dbm
pow -10 dbm
pow -20 dbm
output off

   Tone in ffts3:2  screen #8  near 2100 MHz

For analog coherence tests the reference tone needs to account for
an LO offset of  -8.80 Hz  and can be configured with:

[oper@cc-apex ~]$ remoteAnritsuMG.py freq 3042.98999120 mhz pow 9.0 dbm output on

In digital coherence tests (Mark6 recording) the tone can be checked with:

oper@recorder3:~> m5tone.py /mark6-3_fuse/1/<scan name>.vdif VDIF_8192-2048-8-2 tmp 1 50e-3 19e6 102400 0 +8.80

=== 86G + 260G dual freq VLBI ===

The HMs are 
 - N3AR HM tone generator assembly with built-in amplifier (spans N3AR & NFLASH freqs, >60 GHz)
 - Pavelyev "230G" WR-3 HM for offsetted 2nd tone at 260G (only NFLASH freqs, >100 GHz),
   Note, 10+3 dB attenuators of HM removed! The needed ~4m long LMR400 coax has 6 dB measured
   cable loss. The HM needs ca 2 dBm. With 13 dB attenuators the Agilent synth setting would be >=21 dBm
   which leads to "output unleveled" in our particular synth.

Careful with power levels!

Command sequence for http://10.0.6.230/scpi to enable dual band tones on N3AR HM:

pow -20 dbm
freq 17218 mhz
output on
pow -10 dbm
pow -5 dbm
pow -2 dbm
pow 0 dbm

Disable tone:

pow -5 dbm
pow -10 dbm
pow -20 dbm
output off


For analog coherence tests the reference tone needs to account for LO offsets of 24.3 Hz (86G) and 75.60 Hz (260G):

[oper@cc-apex ~]$ remoteAnritsuMG.py freq 2130.98997570 mhz pow 9.0 dbm output on  # N3AR/86G
[oper@cc-apex ~]$ remoteAnritsuMG.py freq 2130.98992440 mhz pow 9.0 dbm output on  # NFLASH/260G

In digital coherence tests (Mark6 recording) the tones can be checked with:

oper@recorder3> m5tone.py /mark6-3_fuse/1/<scan name>.vdif VDIF_8192-4096-1-2 tmp 1 50e-3 249e6 819200 0 -24.30  # N3AR/86G
oper@recorder2> m5tone.py /mark6-2_fuse/1/<scan name>.vdif VDIF_8192-4096-1-2 tmp 1 50e-3 249e6 819200 0 -75.60  # NFLASH/260G


Command sequence for http://10.0.6.249/scpi to enable secondary tone of 230G WR-3 HM,
note, removed(!) the 10+3 dB attenuators:

pow -20 dbm
freq 17219.20 mhz
output on
pow -10 dbm
pow 0 dbm
pow 4 dbm
pow 8 dbm

Disable tone:

pow -5 dbm
pow -10 dbm
pow -20 dbm
output off

In digital coherence tests (Mark6 recording) this 2nd nflash tone can be checked with:

oper@recorder2> m5tone.py /mark6-2_fuse/1/<scan name>.vdif VDIF_8192-4096-1-2 tmp 1 50e-3 252e6 819200 0 -75.60
